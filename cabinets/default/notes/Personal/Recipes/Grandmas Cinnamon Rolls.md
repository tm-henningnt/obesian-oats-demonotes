## Bestemors Kanelboller (Grandma's Cinnamon Rolls)

This is grandma Astrid's original recipe, handwritten in her blue notebook. Makes ~20 rolls.

### Dough

- 500ml whole milk (lukewarm, not hot!)
- 50g fresh yeast
- 150g sugar
- 1 tsp cardamom (her secret: freshly ground, not pre-ground)
- 750g all-purpose flour
- 100g butter (melted, cooled)
- 1 egg
- Pinch of salt

### Filling

- 100g soft butter
- 3 tbsp cinnamon
- 100g sugar
- Optional: 50g finely chopped almonds (grandma's addition for Christmas)

### Topping

- 1 egg (beaten, for brushing)
- Pearl sugar

### Method

1. Dissolve yeast in lukewarm milk. Add sugar and let sit 5 minutes
2. Mix in cardamom, half the flour, melted butter, egg, and salt
3. Gradually add remaining flour — dough should be slightly sticky but workable
4. Knead 10 minutes until smooth and elastic
5. Cover with kitchen towel, rise 45-60 minutes until doubled
6. **Grandma's tip:** "Don't rush the rising. Put the bowl near the radiator."
7. Roll out to a large rectangle (~40x60cm)
8. Spread soft butter, sprinkle cinnamon-sugar mixture evenly
9. Roll up tightly from the long side
10. Cut into ~3cm thick slices
11. Place on baking sheet with parchment, space 3cm apart
12. Cover and rise 30 minutes
13. Brush with beaten egg, sprinkle pearl sugar
14. Bake at 225°C for 10-12 minutes — **do not overbake!**
15. Cool on wire rack under a towel to keep them soft

### Notes from Grandma

*"The secret is good butter and patience. And never let the milk get too hot — you'll kill the yeast and the rolls will be flat as lefser."*

*"Always make double. They disappear before they cool down."*

### My Modifications

- I add a tiny pinch of vanilla sugar to the dough (sorry, bestemor)
- Sometimes drizzle with a simple glaze: powdered sugar + splash of milk
- The almond version at Christmas is non-negotiable
