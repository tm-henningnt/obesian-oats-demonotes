## Sourdough Bread

Weekend baking project. Takes 2 days but mostly waiting. The result is so much better than store-bought.

### Starter

Feed starter 12 hours before mixing: equal parts flour and water (50g each). Should be bubbly and doubled when ready.

### Ingredients

- 450g bread flour
- 50g whole wheat flour
- 350g water (warm, not hot)
- 100g active starter
- 10g salt

### Schedule

**Day 1 (Evening)**
1. Mix flour and water, autolyse 30 min
2. Add starter and salt, pinch and fold to incorporate
3. Stretch and fold every 30 min for 2 hours (4 sets)
4. Bulk ferment at room temp 3-4 hours (until 50% rise)
5. Pre-shape, rest 20 min, final shape
6. Place in banneton, cover, refrigerate overnight

**Day 2 (Morning)**
1. Preheat oven with Dutch oven to 250°C
2. Score cold dough with razor blade
3. Bake covered 20 min at 250°C
4. Uncover, reduce to 230°C, bake 20-25 min more
5. Cool on wire rack at least 1 hour before cutting!

### Notes

- Kitchen temperature matters a LOT — adjust ferment times accordingly
- The "poke test": dough springs back slowly when ready
- Scoring pattern affects how the bread opens — experiment!
- My starter is named "Sven" and he is 2 years old 🍞
